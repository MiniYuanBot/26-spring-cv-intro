\section{Convolutional Neural Networks}
\subsection{CNN Core Mechanisms}
\subsubsection*{Conv Layer}
Input $W_{in}\times H_{in}\times C$; kernel $F\times F\times C$; $K$ filters; stride $S$; padding $P$.
$W_{out}=\lfloor\frac{W_{in}-F+2P}{S}\rfloor+1$; params $=(F^2C+1)K$.

\subsubsection*{Padding and Stride}
Valid: $P=0$. Same: $P=\lfloor\frac{F-1}{2}\rfloor$. Full: $P=F-1$.
Content: zero, constant, reflect, replicate, circular.

\subsubsection*{Pooling}
Types: max (default), average, sum. No trainable params.
Increases invariance to small trans/rotstion.

\subsubsection*{CNN vs FC}
Params: Conv $(F^2C+1)K$ vs FC $(W_{in}H_{in}C+1)(W_{out}H_{out}K)$.
Sparse connectivity + parameter sharing give CNN translation equivariance and location preservation.
MLP $\supseteq$ CNN in expressive power; CNN adds \textbf{inductive bias} for vision.

\subsection{CNN Training Practice}
\subsubsection*{Data Preprocessing}
Normalize: per-channel zero-mean/unit-var or scale to $[0,1]$. Augment: random crop, flip, rotate, color jitter. Shuffle and batch.

\subsubsection*{Weight Initialization}
Preserve variance: $\sigma_w^2=\frac{1}{n_{\text{in}}}$.
Xavier/Glorot (tanh/sigmoid): $\sigma_w^2=\frac{1}{n_{\text{in}}}$ or $\frac{2}{n_{\text{in}}+n_{\text{out}}}$.
He (ReLU): $\sigma_w^2=\frac{2}{n_{\text{in}}}$.
Uniform: $W\sim\mathcal{U}(-\sqrt{3\sigma_w^2},\sqrt{3\sigma_w^2})$.
Gaussian: $W\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma_w^2)$.

\subsubsection*{Batch Training}
Iteration: one forward+backward on a batch. Epoch: full pass over training set.

\subsubsection*{Handle Underfitting}
\subsubsection*{Handle Underfitting}
\textbf{Normalization.}
(1) BatchNorm.
Train: Per channel: $\mu=\frac{1}{NHW}\sum x$, $\sigma^2=\frac{1}{NHW}\sum(x-\mu)^2$, $\hat x=\frac{x-\mu}{\sqrt{\sigma^2+\epsilon}}$, $y=\gamma\hat x+\beta$ ($2C$ params).
Eval: $\mu_{\text{run}}\leftarrow\rho\mu_{\text{run}}+(1-\rho)\mu_B$, $\sigma^2_{\text{run}}$ likewise; inference with running stats.
Before activation, never on output.
(2) MLP: per feature dim over $N$ samples ($2D$ params).
(3) Pros: stabilizes layers (larger LR), smooths landscape, mild reg. Cons: train-eval gap at small batch; assumes i.i.d. spatially, fails for correlated data.
(4) Variants: LayerNorm (per sample); InstanceNorm (per channel per sample); GroupNorm (channel groups). No batch dependence.

\textbf{Residual Link.}
$y=F(\mathbf{x})+\mathbf{x}$. Learn residual; drive $F\to\mathbf{0}$ if depth hurts. Pros: bypass fights vanishing/exploding; flatter landscape.

\subsubsection*{Handle Overfitting}
Match \textbf{data variability} to \textbf{model capacity}.
(1) Early Stopping. Stop when val acc degrades.
(2) Data Augmentation. Input transforms; magnitude critical—too strong induce error/ambiguity, too weak useless.
(3) Regularization. $\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}}=\mathcal{L}_{\text{main}}+R(\theta)$; only $W$ penalized.
L2: $R=\frac{\lambda}{2}\|W\|_2^2$; weight decay $\alpha\lambda$.
L1: $R=\lambda\|W\|_1$; step $\alpha\lambda$ to zero, sparsity.
Elastic Net: $R=\lambda_1\|W\|_1+\frac{\lambda_2}{2}\|W\|_2^2$; both.
(4) Dropout.
Train: mask $\mathbf{r}\sim\text{Bernoulli}(1-p)$, output $\mathbf{r}\odot\mathbf{a}$. Breaks co-adaptation.
Eval: scales outputs by $1-p$.
Inverted Dropout: train scale $1/(1-p)$, eval no-op(preferred).
FC only; BN suffices for Conv.
(5) BN as Regularization. Gaussianizes pre-activations + batch noise, cuts capacity.

\subsection{Classification Backbones}
\subsubsection*{Receptive Field}
Jump: $j_\ell=\prod_{i=1}^{\ell-1}s_i$, $j_1=1$.
Receptive field: $f_\ell=f_{\ell-1}+(k_{\ell-1}-1)j_{\ell-1}$, $f_1=1$.
$k_\ell\ge s_\ell$ so no gaps.

\subsubsection*{VGG}
Smaller kernels, deeper nets. Three $3\times3$ layers = one $7\times7$ RF, but more nonlinearities and fewer params ($27C^2$ vs $49C^2$).

\subsubsection*{ResNet}
Residual block.
Bottleneck: $1\times1$ (reduce), $3\times3$ (spatial), $1\times1$ (restore). Bottleneck params $\ll$ Basic block.

\subsubsection*{Beyond ResNet}
SENet: channel attention. DenseNet: dense connections, concat all previous.
MobileNet: separable conv. depthwise (per-channel) + pointwise ($1\times1$ channel mix). Cost $\approx$ standard/$N$.
NAS: neural architecture search.
